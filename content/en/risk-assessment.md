Risk assessment is the process of identifying the potential or actual [risk](risk.md)s introduced by a system.

## Problem it addresses

Organizations need a structured way to understand what could go wrong before they set [policy](policy.md) or [control](control.md)s.
Without assessment, [risk mitigation](risk-mitigation.md) and [risk appetite](risk-appetite.md) lack a clear basis.

## How it helps

Risk assessment ties [capability](capabilities.md)s, [threat](threat.md)s, and [vulnerability](vulnerability.md)s to [risk](risk.md) so that [organization](organization.md)s can prioritize and respond.
It supports [risk catalog](risk-catalog.md)s and [policy](policy.md) design.

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
