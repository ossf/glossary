Risk acceptance is a clearly documented decision to accept an unmitigated [risk](risk.md) as necessary or unavoidable.

## Problem it addresses

Not every [risk](risk.md) can or should be fully mitigated; some must be explicitly accepted.
Without documentation, accepted [risk](risk.md) can be forgotten or disputed later.

## How it helps

Risk acceptance ties [residual risk](residual-risk.md) and [risk appetite](risk-appetite.md) to a formal decision.
It supports [governance](governance.md) and [audit](audit.md) by making accepted [risk](risk.md) visible and accountable.

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
