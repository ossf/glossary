A catalog is a structured set of related text and metadata, such as [guidance](guidance.md), [control](control.md)s, or [threat](threat.md)s.

## Problem it addresses

Scattered or ad hoc lists of rules, [guideline](guideline.md)s, or [control](control.md)s are hard to maintain and reuse.
Organizations need a consistent way to group and reference related items.

## How it helps

A catalog gives a single place to store and version related content.
It supports reuse across [policy](policy.md), [evaluation](evaluation.md), and tooling and makes it easier to align with standards.

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
