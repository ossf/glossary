A policy is a clearly scoped set of [rule](rule.md)s based on an [organization](organization.md)'s [risk appetite](risk-appetite.md).

## Problem it addresses

Organizations need to state what is allowed or required in a way that fits their tolerance for [risk](risk.md).
Unclear or unbounded rules are hard to enforce and [evaluate](evaluation.md).

## How it helps

Scoping policy and tying it to [risk appetite](risk-appetite.md) makes [compliance](compliance.md) and [audit](audit.md) meaningful.
It supports [control](control.md) design and [enforcement](enforcement.md) by linking [rule](rule.md)s to [organization](organization.md) intent.

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
