Risk appetite is the amount of [risk](risk.md) an [organization](organization.md) is willing to accept in pursuit of its objectives.

## Problem it addresses

Organizations must decide how much [risk](risk.md) they will tolerate so that [policy](policy.md) and [control](control.md)s can be set accordingly.
Without a stated appetite, decisions about [risk acceptance](risk-acceptance.md) and [residual risk](residual-risk.md) are inconsistent.

## How it helps

Defining risk appetite gives a clear basis for [policy](policy.md) scope and [risk assessment](risk-assessment.md).
It supports [governance](governance.md) and [audit](audit.md) by making tolerance explicit.

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
