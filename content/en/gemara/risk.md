Risk is the potential for loss or damage when a [threat](threat.md) is realized, often described by the impact of an event on an [organization](organization.md) and the likelihood of it occurring.

## Problem it addresses

Organizations need a shared way to talk about what could go wrong and how much it matters.
Without a clear idea of risk, [policy](policy.md), [risk assessment](risk-assessment.md), and [risk mitigation](risk-mitigation.md) lack a common basis.

## How it helps

Defining risk in terms of [threat](threat.md) and impact supports [risk appetite](risk-appetite.md), [risk acceptance](risk-acceptance.md), and [residual risk](residual-risk.md) discussions.
It ties [governance](governance.md) and [compliance](compliance.md) to the potential for harm.

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
