An assessment is either the process of checking whether an outcome matches what someone intended, or a single step inside an [evaluation](evaluation.md) that determines whether a resource meets an [assessment requirement](assessment-requirement.md).

## Problem it addresses

Organizations need to know whether their systems and processes actually meet the rules they have set.
A single, repeatable way to answer "did we meet this requirement?" is missing without a clear idea of what an assessment is.

## How it helps

Defining assessment as a process (or an atomic step within evaluation) gives teams a shared way to check [compliance](compliance.md).
It separates the act of judging from the broader [evaluation](evaluation.md) and from the requirements being checked.

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
