Residual risk is the [risk](risk.md) that remains after [risk mitigation](risk-mitigation.md) and [enforcement](enforcement.md) actions have been applied.

## Problem it addresses

Not all [risk](risk.md) can be removed; some remains after [control](control.md)s and [policy](policy.md) are in place.
Organizations need a clear way to talk about what is left and whether it is acceptable.

## How it helps

Naming residual risk supports [risk acceptance](risk-acceptance.md) and [risk appetite](risk-appetite.md) decisions.
It helps [organization](organization.md)s be explicit about what they accept and what they will monitor or improve.

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
