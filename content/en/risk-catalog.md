A risk catalog is a group of related [risk](risk.md)s that are relevant to an [organization](organization.md), used to decide when and how [policy](policy.md) is created.

## Problem it addresses

[Risk](risk.md)s are easier to manage when they are collected and described in one place.
Scattered or ad hoc risk lists make it hard to align [policy](policy.md) and [risk appetite](risk-appetite.md).

## How it helps

A risk catalog supports [risk assessment](risk-assessment.md) and [governance](governance.md) by giving a shared view of what the [organization](organization.md) cares about.
It helps connect [threat](threat.md)s and [vulnerability](vulnerability.md)s to [policy](policy.md) and [control](control.md) decisions.

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
