A vector is (1) an opportunity for an attacker to exploit a [vulnerability](vulnerability.md) in a system, or (2) a path by which neglect could result in unintentional negative outcomes.

## Problem it addresses

To manage [risk](risk.md) and [threat](threat.md)s, organizations must understand how harm can reach a system or outcome.
Vague ideas about "how things go wrong" make [guidance](guidance.md) and [control](control.md) design harder.

## How it helps

Defining vector supports [guidance](guidance.md) and [threat](threat.md) analysis by tying harm to specific paths and [capability](capabilities.md)s.
It helps connect [vulnerability](vulnerability.md)s and [threat](threat.md)s to [risk mitigation](risk-mitigation.md) and [policy](policy.md).

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
