A threat is a situation or event in which the ideas behind a [vector](vector.md) are applied to a [capability](capability.md) in a specific context, with the potential for harm.

## Problem it addresses

To manage [risk](risk.md), organizations must understand what can go wrong and in what context.
Vague or disconnected ideas about harm make [risk assessment](risk-assessment.md) and [control](control.md) design harder.

## How it helps

Defining threat as the application of [vector](vector.md) concepts to [capability](capability.md)s ties [risk](risk.md) to concrete system features and contexts.
It supports [risk mitigation](risk-mitigation.md), [policy](policy.md), and threat [catalog](catalog.md)s.

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
