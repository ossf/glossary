Risk mitigation is the process of defining and taking actions to prevent [threat](threat.md)s or reduce their impact on [organization](organization.md) objectives.

## Problem it addresses

Identifying [risk](risk.md) is not enough; organizations must decide what to do about it.
Without a clear idea of mitigation, [risk assessment](risk-assessment.md) does not lead to [control](control.md)s or [policy](policy.md).

## How it helps

Risk mitigation connects [threat](threat.md)s and [vulnerability](vulnerability.md)s to [enforcement](enforcement.md) and [control](control.md) design.
It supports [residual risk](residual-risk.md) and [risk acceptance](risk-acceptance.md) by making it clear what has been addressed and what remains.

Source: [Gemara: A Governance, Risk, and Compliance Engineering Model for Automated Risk Assessment](https://openssf.org/resources/gemara-a-governance-risk-and-compliance-engineering-model-for-automated-risk-assessment/)
